Judas Cradle

Description:

  • A pyramid shaped pillar that is pointed to the victims anus. Using ropes your feet and hands are bound and you are held by a belt wrapped around your ribcage.

What it does and why it was used:

  • In medieval and early modern times this was used as a means of punishment and coercion to extract confessions. The device sets you on a peak of a pyramid shape pillar, usually out of wood but can be out of metal. The weight of the victims drives them further down the peak and the suffering is prolonged and worsen. In addition, the device was usually rocked and twisted. The length of time spent on this device led to your death.

Who died from it:

  • During the Spanish inquisition it was the preferred method to force confessions from people who were charged with witchcraft or skepticism.
  • Used to torture both political dissidents and regular crimenals.
