The Spanish Donkey

Description:

  • Long triangular prism that sits on four legs making it look like a body of a quadruped. On the spine of the prism there are spikes on which the victim gets straddled with weights at their angles and a noose on their neck.

What it does and why it was used:

  • It was used during the spanish inquisition and throughout the 16th and 17th century and the spanish inquisition. It was also famously used by Jesuits, Civil War soldiers, and Paul Revere. Victims were stripped from their clothing and straddled over the device. They would be tickled sometimes to make pain more severe. This device would bleed out the victims or they would die from excruciating pain or in some cases be split in two.

Art that was made:

Who died from it:

  • Nonbeliever during the Spanish inquisition.
